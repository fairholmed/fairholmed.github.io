The Importance of Mobile Connectivity @Home

Traditionally home buyers and renters have considered school catchment areas, the quality of local amenities and crime statistics, when viewing prospective properties. However, with people now so reliant on their mobile phone, network signal strength within a property is a significant consideration.

Ofcom research shows that more than two thirds of people own smartphones and that the smartphone has now overtaken the laptop, as the primary device for getting on-line. This also means that for many people, at home, their smartphone also becomes their primary point of contact, with fixed line phones becoming obsolete. With so many people reliant on their smartphone for work, social media, messaging, on-line shopping and so many other things, it is no wonder that mobile signal has become such a high consideration, when choosing a new home.

Technology Considerations

Mobile technologies are quickly evolving, as the demand for faster and higher quality connectivity increases. 2G, 3G, 4G and 4G+ are all currently available in the UK and 5G will arrive soon. Knowing how many bars a phone displays is not enough to understand what the connected experience will be like at a property. The 5App HomeSurveyor tool takes mobile technology and a number of other measurements into consideration, when measuring mobile signal strength.

Don't Guess .... Measure

Mobile operators provide coverage maps, however, these tend to be based on predicted coverage, rather than actual measurements. Even using independent coverage maps is not recommended, as most of the measurements are from outdoor coverage data and indoor coverage can be significantly different.
