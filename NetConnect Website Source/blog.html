Providing a True End to End Customer Experience Solution in Today's Market

Posted by Damian Fairholme

With so many companies claiming to provide end to end network monitoring, or an end to end customer service solution, what is the truth? These companies say that using network stats, faults, probing, or a number of other feeds by themselves can provide this. Some combine two, three or maybe more of these to provide their version of a complete solution.

To see the full customer experience picture, a jigsaw of feeds must be completed first. These include performance counters, incidents, probing, device data and much more, along with a flexible reporting and analytics layer. All of these have various uses and come from different sources. They also have their own challenges when integrating within reporting, analytics, security, connectivity, data processing, legality, size and performance.

The challenge of pulling all of this together is fourfold. Firstly, find a good quality solution vendor with a tried and tested product. Secondly, a knowledgeable stakeholder in the CSP is required to gain project commitment and drive the correct level of technical integration. Thirdly, pulling together the teams necessary to give access and provide an understanding of the data being collected. Finally, getting element vendors and system vendors, such as the ticketing and planning systems, to provide these feeds in real-time and in a consistent manner.

MA key requirement in most cases is to go directly to the element managers or systems that generate the data, and not to use multiple underlying systems to build up an umbrella solution. This will provide as close to a real time collection as possible, and moves the aggregation and summarised data inside the solution. End users can then see exactly how a high level dashboard or report is built up, drilling down into the raw data where necessary, and ensures a clean data feed.

The complexity of providing good quality data feeds, reading and processing them in real time, and providing meaningful reports, dashboards and analytics over the top is a major challenge. It can take a long time and a staged rollout is recommended. Configuration of each feed correctly is necessary to see the benefits as the project progresses. This requires a strong and consistent management across the project so focus is not lost.

Another major challenge is to handle existing stakeholders within the business who have strong views and feelings regarding the incumbent tools within the organisation. When replacing an existing solution obtaining buy-in from the affected management team, and a clear strategy to swap out the existing tool set, is vital.

Bringing all of this together is possible, and with the correct management team, analytics tool and a good quality tool vendor, a true end-to-end customer experience solution can be delivered.
